Closing the loop for heavy metal waste

Electronic manufacturing consists of many processes, each of which generate wastewater with a unique composition of heavy metals.

The most common treatment method is to precipitate the heavy metals out into a toxic sludge that poses major health risks to humans and wildlife.

One process used in the industry, called metal-finishing, produces approximately 150,000 tons of this sludge in the European Union alone every year.*

*Magalhães, J.M., Silva, J. E., Castro, F., & Labrincha, J.A. (2005). Physical and Chemical Characterisation of Metal Finishing Industrial Wastes. Journal of Environmental Management, 75(2),157-66. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jenvman.2004.09.011

But what if we could recover these metals for reuse?

REMINE uses metal-binding proteins to specifically extract valuable metals in a packed column.

The wastewater is now metal-free and ready to be discharged safely into the environment.

By then breaking the bond between the protein and the metal ion, REMINE can produce a pure concentrated metal solution that can be re-used in the industry.
